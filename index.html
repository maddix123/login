1. Why Passport Photos Get Rejected (Detailed Research Explanation)

Governments follow biometric guidelines issued under international aviation and identity security standards. These standards are designed to ensure:

  • Facial recognition compatibility
  • Uniform database storage
  • Border security scanning accuracy
  • Identity fraud prevention

Because passport photos are used for machine-based identification, they must meet precise requirements.

Most Common Global Rejection Reasons

  • Incorrect dimensions (wrong width or height)
  • Improper head size ratio
  • Head tilted or rotated
  • Shadows on the face
  • Background not plain white or light neutral
  • Glasses worn when not allowed
  • Visible teeth in smile
  • Hair covering eyes
  • Low resolution printing
  • Over-editing with filters

Even professionally taken studio photos are sometimes rejected due to incorrect cropping or printing errors.

3. Passport Photo Size Requirements by Country (In-Depth Overview)

Different countries use different dimensions and head ratio standards. Using the wrong template is one of the top causes of rejection.

United States Passport Photo Size

  • 2 x 2 inches (51mm x 51mm)
  • Head height must be between 50% and 69% of total photo height
  • White or off-white background

United Kingdom Passport Photo Size

  • 35mm x 45mm
  • Head height between 29mm and 34mm
  • Strict neutral expression required

Canada Passport Photo Size

  • 50mm x 70mm
  • Face length between 31mm and 36mm

India Passport Photo Size

  • 35mm x 45mm
  • Plain white background

Uganda & Kenya Passport Photo Size

  • Commonly 45mm x 45mm (varies depending on document type)
  • Some visas require 35mm x 45mm format

This variation explains why generic resizing apps often fail.

6. Advanced Mistakes That Cause Hidden Rejections
